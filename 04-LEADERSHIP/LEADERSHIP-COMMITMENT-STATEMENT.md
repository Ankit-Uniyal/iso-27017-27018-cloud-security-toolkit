# Leadership Commitment Statement — Cloud Information Security & PII Protection

**Document ID:** CLOUD-LCS-001 | **Version:** 1.0 | **Classification:** Public | **Owner:** CEO | **Review:** Annual

## Statement

[Organisation Name] depends on cloud services to deliver value to our customers. We commit to protecting the confidentiality, integrity and availability of all information processed in the cloud — including the personally identifiable information (PII) entrusted to us by our customers — in accordance with ISO/IEC 27001:2022, ISO/IEC 27017:2015 and ISO/IEC 27018:2019.

## Top-management commitments (per ISO/IEC 27001:2022 cl. 5.1)

Top management commits to:

1. **Establish** a Cloud Information Security Policy and information-security objectives compatible with strategic direction.
2. **Integrate** cloud-ISMS requirements into business processes — including procurement, engineering, sales and customer support.
3. **Ensure resources** — financial, human, technical — are available for the Cloud ISMS.
4. **Communicate** the importance of effective cloud-information-security management and conformance to ISMS requirements.
5. **Ensure the ISMS achieves** its intended outcomes.
6. **Direct and support persons** to contribute to the ISMS effectiveness.
7. **Promote continual improvement.**
8. **Support other relevant management roles** (CISO, DPO, Cloud Security Officer) to demonstrate leadership.
9. **Comply** with applicable laws and regulations of every jurisdiction we operate in.
10. **Protect PII** of data subjects regardless of role (controller or processor) — applying ISO/IEC 27018 controls.
11. **Honour customer-instruction limits** — PII processed only as instructed; never used for our own commercial purposes.
12. **Maintain transparency** about our sub-processors and security posture.

## Cloud-Specific Commitments

- Maintain certification (or equivalent assurance) for ISO/IEC 27001 / 27017 / 27018.
- Operate the shared-responsibility model in good faith with our cloud service providers and customers.
- Notify customers of any PII-affecting incidents within contractually agreed SLAs.
- Provide customers with the means to exercise data-subject rights.
- Restrict cross-border PII transfers to lawful mechanisms.
- Apply privacy-by-design and security-by-design in every cloud service we build.

## Resourcing

Top management approves the Cloud ISMS budget annually, including funding for the Risk Treatment Plan, the Internal Audit Programme, training, tooling and external assurance.

## Review

This statement is reviewed at least annually by the CEO and Board, and reissued as required.
